🏆 How The Sweep Works

The Draw

All 48 World Cup teams were ranked by TAB outright odds and split into 4 tiers of 11 (the 4 longest-odds teams — Cabo Verde, Jordan, Curaçao, Haiti — were excluded from the draw). Each of the 11 participants was randomly allocated one team from each tier, giving everyone 4 teams of mixed strength. See the 🎟️ Draw & Odds tab for the full breakdown, including a Live Odds table sourced from Polymarket that updates periodically as the tournament progresses.

Reading the Scorecards

On the 📊 Scorecards tab, a ⏳ in any round column means that team's fixture for that round is scheduled but hasn't been played yet — a plain – means the round doesn't apply or hasn't been reached. Each person's Next Match box also shows who they're effectively up against in the sweep for that fixture, where applicable.

🔍 Analysis Tools

🎲 Win %

Runs a Monte Carlo simulation (1,500 simulated tournaments) using live Polymarket implied odds as team strength (falling back to draw-day TAB odds if the live feed is unavailable), replaying the remaining group and knockout stages each time to estimate every participant's chance of winning the sweep outright. Recalculates automatically whenever live scores or odds change — not on every visit — so it loads instantly most of the time.

⚡ Impact

Pick any upcoming match involving a sweep team and see exactly how the leaderboard would shift under each possible result — Win / Draw / Loss — with rank-movement arrows showing who climbs or falls.

⚔️ H2H

Compare any two participants directly: total points, current rank, a team-by-team tier breakdown, and a round-by-round scoring comparison.

📡 How Data Flows

Live results, zero manual entry

Every time this page loads — or you tap ↻ Refresh — the dashboard fetches the full match schedule and results directly from openfootball/worldcup.json, a free, publicly maintained dataset. As results come in, scores update automatically.

Points calculated automatically

For each team, the dashboard works out their 1st, 2nd and 3rd group matches in chronological order and assigns 3/1/0 points based on the result. Once all 6 matches in a group are finished, group winner/runner-up bonuses are awarded automatically (ties broken by points → goal difference → goals scored, per standard FIFA rules). Knockout wins, the Final, and the 3rd-place match are scored the same way the moment results land.

Knockout placeholders

Before the group stage finishes, knockout fixtures show placeholder codes (e.g. 1A = Group A winner, W74 = winner of Match 74). These automatically resolve to real team names and flags as soon as the relevant results are in.

Times & "Scores Updated"

All match times are shown in Melbourne (AEST). The Scores updated timestamp in the header reflects the moment the dashboard last successfully pulled live results from the data feed — it updates every time the page loads or you tap ↻ Refresh, independent of when the underlying code was last deployed.
